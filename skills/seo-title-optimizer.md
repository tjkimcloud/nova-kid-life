# Skill: SEO Title Optimizer

**Activation:** Apply when writing or auditing page titles and H1 headings. Not a full-page SEO audit — use `seo-technical.md` for that.

**Reference file:** `skills/title-formulas.md` — 10+ proven headline templates for local family event content.

---

## Process

1. **Identify the primary keyword cluster** — cross-reference `seo-content-strategy.md` sections 2–3 for the relevant cluster
2. **Choose a title formula** from `title-formulas.md` that matches the content type
3. **Draft 3 variants** applying the formula to the specific content
4. **Count characters** on each variant — must land in 40–60 chars
5. **Check geographic signal** — every title must include VA, Virginia, NoVa, or a specific NoVa county/city
6. **Pick the variant** with the highest local intent signal AND most specific keyword
7. **Write the H1** — can be slightly longer than the title tag (H1 is on-page, not truncated in SERP), but should match the same keyword intent

| Blog post | `{Primary Keyword} ({Year}) — NovaKidLife` |
| Location page | `Family Events in {City}, VA \| NovaKidLife` |

---

## Quick Character-Count Targets

- Title tag: **40–60 characters** (hard cap at 60)
- H1 (on-page): **40–70 characters** (no pixel truncation on-page)
- Meta description: **140–155 characters** (see `seo-technical.md` section 2)

---

## Optimization Signals (rank these in order)

1. Geographic specificity — city-level beats county-level beats "Northern Virginia"
2. Intent match — transactional ("register") beats informational ("learn about") for event pages
3. Freshness signal — include year or month for seasonal/list content
4. Parent voice — "for kids" and "with kids" convert better than "children's"
5. Free signal — "free" in the title drives CTR for parents regardless of other factors

---

## Quality Check Before Finalizing

- [ ] 40–60 characters (use a char counter)
- [ ] Geographic signal present
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 5 words when possible
- [ ] No keyword stuffing (reads naturally when said aloud)
- [ ] Unique — does not duplicate another page's title
- [ ] Matches the H1 intent (can differ in wording, not in topic)
