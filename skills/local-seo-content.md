## 1. Local Link Building — Authority That Compounds

Links from locally relevant sites carry more local SEO weight than generic DA links.

### Priority Link Targets (ranked by local authority)

**Government and institutional (highest authority)**
- Fairfax County Government (fairfaxcounty.gov) — community resources, events calendar
- Loudoun County Government (loudoun.gov) — family resources
- Arlington County (arlingtonva.us) — community links
- Fairfax County Public Library (fairfaxcounty.gov/library) — link as a community resource
- Loudoun County Public Library (library.loudoun.gov) — same
- NOVA Parks (novaparks.com) — link as an events discovery resource
- Virginia Tourism Corporation (virginia.org) — family events section

**Local education and school networks**
- FCPS (Fairfax County Public Schools) — parent resources page
- LCPS (Loudoun County Public Schools) — same
- Homeschool networks — high-value NoVa parent community
- Local PTAs/PTOs — their websites often link out to community resources

**Local organizations and nonprofits**
- Reston Community Center (restoncommunitycenter.com) — already a scrape source, become a partner
- YMCA Northern Virginia branches
- Wolf Trap Foundation (wolftrap.org) — cultural authority
- Meadowlark Botanical Gardens
- Claude Moore Colonial Farm
- Cox Farms — very popular with NoVa families
- George Mason University — arts/events partner

**Local business associations**
- Fairfax County Chamber of Commerce
- Loudoun Chamber of Commerce
- Reston Chamber of Commerce
- Northern Virginia Technology Council (NVTC)

**Local parenting communities (high relevance)**
- dullesmoms.com — pitch as a resource, offer to contribute content
- DCUrbanMom.com — DC metro parent forum, very active
- NoVa Moms Group (Facebook) — link in profile or as a resource
- Dulles South Moms (Facebook) — same


### Link Building Cadence

- Month 1–2: Citations and GBP (foundation)
- Month 3–4: Outreach to libraries, county sites, community orgs (institutional links)
- Month 5–6: Local press pitches, parenting blogs (authority links)
- Ongoing: GBP posts, fresh content, social shares (freshness signals)

## 2. Blog Local SEO Strategy

The blog captures long-tail local searches that event pages can't target.

### Blog Content Pillars

**Pillar A — Location Guides (city-level authority)**

Format: "X Best [Activity] for Kids in [City], VA"

Examples:
- "15 Best Free Things to Do with Kids in Reston, VA"
- "12 Best Indoor Playgrounds Near Fairfax, VA"
- "Top 10 Kid-Friendly Hiking Trails in Loudoun County, VA"
- "7 Best Birthday Party Venues for Kids in Chantilly, VA"
- "Free Storytime Locations in Northern Virginia (Complete Guide)"

SEO structure:
- H1: `{Number} Best {Activity} for Kids in {City}, VA`
- H2 per item: `{Venue Name} — {City}` (exact name for entity matching)
- Include: full address, hours, age range, cost, parking, tips from a parent POV
- Link to: relevant events on NovaKidLife, venue's official site
- Schema: `ItemList` + `LocalBusiness` per venue

**Pillar B — Seasonal Guides (time-sensitive, high search volume)**

Format: "Best [Season] Activities for Kids in Northern Virginia [Year]"

Examples:
- "Best Fall Family Events in Northern Virginia 2026"
- "Free Holiday Events in NoVa This December"
- "Best Summer Camps in Fairfax County 2026 (By Age)"
- "Spring Break Activities for Kids in Northern Virginia 2026"
- "Halloween Events for Families in NoVa 2026"

Key tactics:
- Publish 6–8 weeks before the season starts
- **Update the same URL each year** — do NOT create new URLs (accumulates link equity)
- Include "Last Updated: [date]" for freshness
- Include the year in the title (parents search "2026 summer camps")

**Pillar C — "Best of" Lists (evergreen, highly linkable)**

These get shared in Facebook groups, Nextdoor, and linked from other sites.

Examples:
- "The Complete Guide to Birthday Freebies in Northern Virginia"
- "Kids Eat Free: Every Restaurant Deal in NoVa (Updated Weekly)"
- "Best Free Kids Events in Northern Virginia This Month"
- "Northern Virginia's Best Farmer's Markets for Families"
- "Every Pokémon TCG League Location in Northern Virginia"

Key tactics:
- Make these the most comprehensive version on the internet
- Update monthly (or auto-update from scraper data)
- Target "NoVa mom" Facebook group share potential — write for the shareable headline
- Schema: `ItemList`, `FAQPage`

**Pillar D — How-To and Informational (top-of-funnel)**

Examples:
- "How to Find Free Kids Events in Northern Virginia"
- "How Pokémon TCG League Nights Work (For NoVa Parents)"
- "How to Get into Events at the Fairfax County Library"

**Pillar E — News/Trending (Perplexity and Google News signals)**

- "New Pokémon TCG Set Releases: Where to Buy in Northern Virginia"
- "[Event] Coming to Northern Virginia in [Month]"
- "Fairfax County Library Just Added These New Storytimes"

### Blog Post SEO Template

```
URL:     /blog/{primary-keyword}-{city}-va       e.g. /blog/free-kids-events-fairfax-va
Title:   {Primary keyword} ({Year}) — NovaKidLife
H1:      Same as title without brand
H2s:     Secondary keywords, category names, venue names
Length:  1,500–3,000 words for pillar; 800–1,200 for supporting posts
Images:  At least 1 per section, all with keyword-rich alt text
Links:   3–5 internal links to event pages; 2–3 external links to authoritative sources
Schema:  Article + FAQPage (if FAQs included) + ItemList (if it's a list)
Update:  Mark posts with "Last Updated" date visible on page
```

### Blog Internal Linking Architecture

```
Blog post (location guide)
    └── links to 5+ individual event pages matching that location
    └── links to events listing filtered by that location (/events?location=reston)
    └── links to related blog posts (seasonal, category)

Event detail page
    └── links back to relevant blog post ("More things to do in Reston →")
    └── links to RelatedEvents component (other events in same city)

Homepage
    └── links to 3 featured blog posts (most linked, most seasonal)
```

**Rule:** Every blog post must link to at least 3 event pages. Every event page must link to at least 1 blog post.

### Blog Publishing Cadence

| Month | Priority content |
|-------|-----------------|
| Launch | 5 pillar posts: one per neighborhood (Fairfax, Reston, Leesburg, Arlington, Manassas) |
| Month 2 | Seasonal guide (next upcoming season) + 2 "Best of" lists |
| Month 3 | 5 city guides for secondary cities + birthday freebies complete guide |
| Monthly ongoing | 1 seasonal update + 1 "Best of" update + 2 new location/category posts |

| Prince William | Manassas, Woodbridge, Dale City, Gainesville, Haymarket, Lake Ridge |

### Location Landing Pages

Create `/locations/[city-slug]` pages for the top 15 NoVa cities. Each page:
- H1: `Family Events in {City}, VA`
- Auto-pulls events filtered by that location from the API
- 200-word intro mentioning neighborhood landmarks
- Links to relevant blog posts for that city
- LocalBusiness schema with `areaServed` = that specific city
- Internal links to/from event pages in that city

### Neighborhood Name-Dropping in Content

Every event page description should mention the neighborhood by name:
- Not: "Join us for storytime"
- Yes: "Join us for Saturday Storytime at Fairfax County Library's Chantilly branch — conveniently located near Westfield Chantilly"

## 4. Local Search Intent and Query Types

### "Near Me" Search Optimization

- Google resolves "near me" based on GBP location + device GPS — GBP setup is the fix
- In content, use "near [landmark]" phrases: "near Reston Town Center", "near Tysons Corner"
- Add GeoCoordinates schema to location pages (see `local-seo-schema.md`)

### Voice Search Optimization

Voice queries are longer and more conversational. Optimize by:
- Writing FAQ sections that directly answer these questions
- Using conversational language in descriptions
- Targeting featured snippets (position 0) — voice assistants read these aloud
- Keeping FAQ answers 40–50 words — the ideal featured snippet length

Example voice queries to target:
- "What are some free things to do with kids this weekend in Fairfax?"
- "Find me a storytime near me for toddlers"
- "When is the next Pokémon TCG event in Manassas?"

### Time-Sensitive Search Strategy

Parents search with time intent: "this weekend", "today", "tomorrow", "this Saturday"
- FilterBar on the frontend already handles this
- Add date-specific content to GBP posts ("This Saturday: storytime at Fairfax Library")
- Event schema `startDate` tells Google exactly when events are

### Zero-Click Optimization

Many local searches end without a click — optimize for zero-click brand impression:
- Event schema appears in search results with date/location/price
- FAQ schema triggers accordion in search results
- GBP shows event info directly in map pack
- Strategy: even if no click, a parent who sees NovaKidLife's name builds brand recognition for the next search
